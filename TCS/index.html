About

Tucson Code school was founded to provide free education to kids who are curious about coding. There is an incredible void in public schools for one of the most important skills anyone can have.

Coding will be as essential as reading in the next few decades. We want to teach the importance of coding as well as the best practices early so the next generation can take control of their future.

TCS is for kids aged 12 and up. We are currently awaiting approval from the Pima County Public Libraries for a meeting room at Eckstrom-Columbus Library. Tentative class schedule will be every Friday from 3:00pm - 4:00pm beginning March 4th.

Founder

What is coding?

You may have seen the craze for learning code. But what exactly is coding? Coding is what makes it possible for us to create computer software, apps and websites. Your browser, your Operating System, the apps on your phone, Facebook, and this website – they’re all made with code. Coding is becoming an essential skill, just like reading, writing, and math. Learning to code can open up countless opportunities for anyone's future.
